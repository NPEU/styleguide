The idea is that smaller numbers of items cause the most visual jarring. They need balance.

If there are more than, say 12 items, then then things will balance out more, but under that number things look a bit weird.

For example:

  • 2 items should be stacked or side-by-side (row). That's fairly easy.
  • 3 items should be probably be the same, though 2--1 is possible - it doesn't look great.
  • 4 items should be stacked, 2--2, or row
  • 5 items should be stacked, 2--2--1, 3--2, or row. 4--1 is possible but looks bad / imbalanced.
  • 6 items should be stacked, 2--2--2, 3--3, or row. (NOT 4--2, 5--1)
  • 7 items should be stacked, 2--2--2--1, 3--3--1, or row (NOT 6--1).
  • 8 items should be stacked, 2--2--2--2, 3--3--2, 4--4 or row (NOT 5--3, 6--2, 7--1)
  • 9 items should be stacked, 2--2--2--2--1, 3--3--3, 5--4 or row (NOT 4--4--1, 6--3, 7--2 or 8--1)
  • 10 items should stacked or 2--2--2--2--2, 3--3--3--1, 5--5, or row (NOT 4--4--2, 6--4, 7--3, 8--2, 9--1)
